Chicken Kabab

Description

An easy and delicious Chicken Kebab recipe. This recipe includes a tasty yoghurt marinade.
These chicken kebab skewers are so versatile, they can be grilled, baked in the oven or cooked on the BBQ.
A new healthy version of your favourite Greek takeaway.

Ingredients

  • 4 skinless chicken breasts (cut into bite-size chunks)
  • 2 red peppers (cut into chunks)
  • 1 red onion (cut into chunks)
  • 4 flatbreads or pittas
  • Chilli sauce and shredded lettuce (to serve)

Preparation

  1. 0 Marinate the Chicken:

  • In a mixing bowl, combine the following ingredients to create the marinade:
  • Greek yogurt (or hung curd)
  • Lemon juice
  • Ginger garlic paste
  • Turmeric
  • Chili powder
  • Salt
  • Oil (olive oil or sesame oil)
  • Garam masala (or a mix of ground cumin, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, pepper, and coriander)
  • Add boneless chicken pieces (make sure they are dry) and sliced onions to the marinade.
  • Refrigerate the mixture for at least 4 hours or overnight for maximum flavor.

    1. 1 Chicken Preparation:

  • Use boneless chicken (about 500 grams) and ensure its dry.
  • Add the chicken to the marinade, along with some finely chopped onions.
  • Coat the chicken pieces thoroughly with the marinade.
  • Refrigerate the marinated chicken for at least 4 hours (overnight is even better).

    1. 2 Grilling or Baking:

  • Preheat your oven to 240¬∞C (464¬∞F) for 15-20 minutes.
  • Rinse skewers and alternate threading chicken and onion pieces onto them.
  • Place the skewers on a baking tray.
  • Grill the chicken kebabs in the preheated oven:

      • First, grill for 15-20 minutes.
      Flip the skewers and grill for another 10-15 minutes.
      For extra flavor, move the tray to the top rack during the last 5 minutes.
      Optionally, brush with a little butter or oil.
      The onions will char and add a delicious flavor.

    1. 3 Serving:

    • Serve the chicken kebabs hot with mint chutney, mint yogurt raita, or green chutney.
    • Enjoy these succulent kebabs as an appetizer or alongside biryani, pulao, wraps, sandwiches, or salads.

    Tawa Mutton

    Description

    Tawa Mutton or Tawa Mutton Fry is an easy to make, lip smacking recipe wherein the
    meat is pre-boiled and then pan fried on a tawa with a mix of spicy gravy.

    Ingredients

    • 1 kg boneless mutton chunks
    • 8 tsp ginger garlic paste
    • 8 tsp brown onion paste
    • 7 tsp oil
    • 3 ¬Ω tsp salt
    • 7 tbsp desi ghee
    • 1 cup chopped onion
    • 8 tsp chopped garlic
    • ¬Ω tbsp chopped ginger
    • 1 tbsp chopped green chilies
    • 200 grams chopped tomatoes
    • 2 tbsp Kashmiri chili powder
    • 2 tbsp coriander powder
    • 1 tsp cumin powder
    • 1 tsp black pepper powder
    • Garam masala powder
    • 20 grams chopped onion for garnish

    Preparation

    1. 0 Marinate the chicken:

  • Take chicken quarter leg pieces (often colloquially called tikka cut).
  • In a bowl, mix yogurt (to make it tender), salt, chili powder (or cayenne for spiciness),
    garam masala (for smoky umami), cumin powder (for earthy depth), and a bit of garlic (for savory flavor).
  • Coat the chicken well with this marinade.

    1. 1 Sear on Tawa

  • Heat a tawa (large skillet) or a flat-bottom pan.
  • Add oil or ghee to the tawa.
  • Place the marinated chicken on the tawa.
  • Sear the chicken on both sides until it gets a smoky depth.

    1. 2 Add Back marinade

  • Pour back the remaining marinade onto the chicken.
  • Let it absorb the flavors and moisture.

    1. 4 Spice It Up:

  • Add thickly sliced green chilies and lemon pieces for extra attitude.
  • Traditionally, serve it inverted onto a large romali roti to soak up all the delicious flavors.

    1. 5 Garnish and Enjoy:

  • Garnish with lemon wedges, cilantro, and ginger.

    Veg Momo

    Description

    A type of dumpling popular in Nepal and Tibet, veg momos are prepared by first making a dough from flour and water, kneading it until smooth and elastic.

    Ingredients

    • 2 cups finely chopped or grated mixed vegetables (e.g., cabbage, carrots, bell peppers, and onions)
    • 1/2 cup finely chopped green onions (scallions)
    • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
    • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon oil (vegetable or sesame oil)
    • Salt to taste
    • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
    • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
    • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)
    • For the dipping sauce (optional):
    • 2 tomatoes, blanched and peeled
    • 2-3 dried red chilies, soaked in warm water
    • 2-3 garlic cloves
    • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
    • 1 tablespoon oil
    • Salt to taste

    Preparation

    1. 1 Prepare the dough:

  • Mix 3/4 cup of all-purpose flour (maida) with a pinch of salt and 1 teaspoon of oil.
  • Gradually add water to form a soft, pliable dough (similar to paratha dough).
  • cover the dough and let it rest for 20-25 minutes.

    1. 2 Make the Vegetable Filling:

  • Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan.
  • Add finely chopped garlic and the white part of spring onion. Saut√© briefly.
  • Add chopped vegetables (carrot, cabbage, capsicum, and green beans) along with salt.
  • Saut√© for 4-5 minutes.
  • Add 1 teaspoon of chili sauce, 1/2-1 teaspoon of soy sauce, and 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper powder. Mix well and turn off the flame.

    1. 3 Assemble the Momos:

  • Knead the dough again and divide it into equal portions.
  • Roll out each portion into a thin disc (approximately 4-5 inches in diameter).
  • Place a spoonful of vegetable filling in the center of each disc.
  • Fold the edges to form a pleated bag shape (potli).
  • Cover them with a damp cloth to prevent drying.
